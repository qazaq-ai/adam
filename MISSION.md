# Mission — Qazaq IR

> **One-line thesis.** Modern AI's three structural problems —
> opacity, cost, hallucination — are not inevitable. Agglutinative
> languages give us a path to a deterministic alternative.

## The three diseases of probabilistic AI

Probabilistic large language models have transformed natural language
processing, but they carry three fundamental problems:

### 1. Black box — opaque probabilistic reasoning

Output is a function of billions of weights tuned on internet-scale
training data. There is no source-attribution mechanism inside the
model: when an LLM says «Алматыда 2 миллион тұрғыны бар», it cannot
point at which document or fact entry produced that number, because
no such pointer exists in the architecture. Post-hoc citation systems
(RAG, search-grounded generation) bolt on attribution but do not
remove the underlying opacity — the generation step is still a
black-box probability distribution.

### 2. Resource cost — GPU and energy

A single GPT-4-class inference call consumes:
- ~1.5 kWh in datacentre energy (≈ household power for 1 hour)
- 16+ GB of VRAM on dedicated GPU
- 50–200 ms latency (best case, with paid priority access)
- Network round-trip to the inference provider

This is acceptable for cloud applications with billions of users
amortising the cost, but it is not deployable on:
- Watch-battery devices
- Offline / regional schools
- Defence environments without external network
- Low-resource languages where the user base does not justify
  per-query cents

### 3. Hallucination risk — confident plausibility

LLMs produce statistically plausible text. They have no internal
mechanism to distinguish claims they have evidence for from claims
they have generated by interpolation. Mitigation is post-hoc — fact
checkers, retrieval augmentation, human review — never architectural.
For high-stakes domains (medicine, law, education, defence), this is
a fundamental obstacle.

## Our hypothesis

**Agglutinative morphology gives a clean algebra of meaning.**

Every Kazakh word decomposes into a root plus a sequence of typed
suffixes: case, number, tense, person, possessive, polarity, modality.
Each suffix contributes a known grammatical operator. Composition is
rule-bound, not learned.

This structure is exactly what we need for a deterministic runtime:

| Property | Probabilistic LLM | Qazaq IR (our approach) |
|---|---|---|
| Internal representation | Continuous weight tensors | Typed morpheme algebra |
| Inference engine | Transformer forward pass | FST + rule-based reasoner |
| Source attribution | None (post-hoc only) | Every claim cites `world_core` |
| Training data | Trillions of tokens | Curated graph of typed facts |
| Hardware footprint | GPU cluster | Single binary on M2 8GB |
| Hallucination control | Post-hoc moderation | Architectural impossibility |
| Reproducibility | Stochastic | Bit-identical given (input, seed) |

We are not claiming Kazakh has special metaphysical status. We are
claiming its structure is unusually friendly to formal modelling.
The same engineering style applies to other agglutinative languages
(Kyrgyz, Tatar, Uzbek, Turkish, Finnish, Hungarian, Japanese,
Korean) — giving the research a global frontier.

## Three goals — and how we measure them

| Goal | Metric | Current state (v5.3.5) |
|---|---|---|
| **Predictability** | Reproducibility of every response from (input, seed, facts) | 100% deterministic on the kernel path |
| **Cheapness** | Memory + CPU + GPU per query | 300 MB RSS / 21 ms p50 / **0% GPU** |
| **Safety** | Hallucination rate (claims without source attribution) | 0% (verified by graph admissibility tests) |

For comparison: Llama-3 8B fp16 inference uses 800–1500 ms per query
with 16 GB VRAM; GPT-4 uses 50–200 ms with hidden datacentre GPU.
Both surface answers without source attribution by default.

## Why Kazakh

Three reasons:

1. **Linguistic clarity.** Kazakh's agglutinative morphology is
   exceptionally regular — 33 letters, mostly 1:1 grapheme-to-phoneme,
   case / tense / person endings that compose deterministically. An
   ideal substrate for formal modelling.

2. **No incumbent.** Anglo-centric and Sino-centric AI giants have
   no strategic reason to investigate non-fusional languages as paths
   to determinism. The space is open. Kazakhstan-local builders have
   the substrate advantage (native intuition for the morphology) plus
   the geographic alignment with the strongest agglutinative-language
   research traditions (Turkic, Finno-Ugric, Mongolic, Korean).

3. **Real users.** Kazakhstan's education and government sectors have
   genuine demand for Kazakh-first AI, providing a real testbed for
   the theoretical work. Research cannot proceed on synthetic
   datasets alone — we need live deployment to surface edge cases.

## adam — the current applied demonstrator

[**adam**](https://github.com/qazaq-ai/adam) is the first applied
proof-of-concept of Qazaq IR. It is a Kazakh-language conversational
assistant currently deployed as a Rust-programming tutor.

What adam demonstrates:

- The deterministic kernel can sustain **multi-turn dialog** with
  belief-state tracking, contradiction recovery, anaphora resolution,
  and curriculum progression.
- The architecture **scales to a real codebase**: 30+ Rust crates,
  1 150+ tests, 451+ versioned releases (each CI-verified).
- The voice surface (macOS `Aru` / Linux `espeak-ng` / optional Piper)
  is a peripheral output transducer — it preserves the kernel's
  determinism guarantee.
- An ordered curriculum (ownership → borrow → lifetime → traits →
  async) with adaptive difficulty driven by real-time student
  performance.

**adam is not the goal.** adam is what proves the architecture works.
The goal is **Qazaq IR** — the deterministic kernel that adam is
built on.

## Roadmap as research arc

Our roadmap is a sequence of research milestones, not feature
releases:

- **Q2 2026 — Demonstrator stability** (current): live REPL deployment,
  multi-turn coherence, voice integration. Status: ✅ shipping.
- **Q3 2026 — Kernel-pure phoneme bank**: replace external TTS with
  hand-recorded native-speaker phoneme units, splicing per the v5.2.0
  G2P module. Removes the last neural dependency from the voice path.
- **Q4 2026 — Multi-language extension**: validate the architecture
  on a second agglutinative language (Kyrgyz or Turkish candidate).
- **2027 — Formal verification**: write down ARK's deterministic
  guarantees as machine-checkable theorems (TLA+ or similar).
- **2027+ — Vertical applications**: FinTech, DefenceTech, HealthTech
  applications of Qazaq IR — the three sectors named in the parent
  Qazna Technologies vision.
